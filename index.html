Plant water-use data: WUCOLS IV — Water Use Classification of Landscape Species (Costello, Matheny & Clark; UC Cooperative Extension, 2014). Dataset: github.com/ucdavis/WUCOLS-plant-search-database — MIT License © 2022 UC Davis. Part of the NeverDry project.

Note on Kc / Kp: WUCOLS IV uses the term plant factor (Kp) in the formula ETc = Kp × ETo, where ETo is the reference evapotranspiration (driven by climate: solar radiation, temperature, humidity, wind) and Kp is a fixed species characteristic. This differs from the FAO-56 crop coefficient Kc (Allen et al., 1998), which varies with crop growth stage. For landscape species, Kp is treated as a constant property of the plant — analogous to a single-stage Kc. This tool labels it Kc for familiarity, with the understanding that the value comes from WUCOLS IV and is fixed per species.
